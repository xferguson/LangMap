# ADR 0004: v1.3 map artifacts cleanup + side panel polish

## Status: Accepted (amends 0001, 0002, 0003)

## Context

v1.2 shipped (ADR 0003): Leaflet renderer on CartoDB Positron tiles, side
panel on the left, 139 languages, 101/101 unit + 13/13 e2e green. Two
rounds of user feedback after the v1.2 manual run:

1. **Stray map lines.** "Horizontal lines drawn on the map at random
   latitudes; long lines cutting north-south over West Africa." A direct
   inspection of `src/data/countries.geo.json` (the file produced by
   `scripts/build-geo.ts` from `world-atlas/countries-50m.json`) confirms
   the geometry-level cause:

   | iso | type         | rings | rings spanning > 180° lon | thinnest sliver ring (lon × lat) |
   | --- | ------------ | ----- | ------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
   | AQ  | MultiPolygon | 109   | 2                         | 358.59° × **0.00°** (257 pts)    |
   | RU  | MultiPolygon | 99    | 2                         | (Chukotka, full antimeridian)    |
   | FJ  | MultiPolygon | 20    | 1                         | 360.00° × 0.11° (10 pts)         |
   | NZ  | MultiPolygon | 13    | 0 (max ring span 7.9°)    | clean                            |
   | US  | MultiPolygon | 127   | 0 (Alaska pre-split)      | clean                            |

   The Antarctica feature includes a 257-point ring that traces the
   southern edge of the projection at a single latitude — under Web
   Mercator (Leaflet's `CRS.EPSG3857`) that ring renders as the
   horizontal band at the bottom of the viewport. The antimeridian-
   crossing rings on AQ, RU, and FJ render as full-width thin strips
   because Leaflet doesn't split them at ±180°. **NZ does not cross the
   antimeridian** in the source dataset — its widest ring spans 7.9°,
   well within a single hemisphere — so no NZ-specific work is needed.

   West Africa contains **no sliver rings or antimeridian crossings** in
   `countries.geo.json` itself (EH, MA, DZ, MR, ML all have clean
   single-hemisphere polygons with thinnest rings spanning > 8° lon × > 4°
   lat). The reported "north-south stray lines" through West Africa are
   the visual tail of Russia's antimeridian-crossing rings: when a Russian
   ring connects (179.9°, 70°N) directly to (-180°, 70°N) Leaflet draws
   that as a polygon edge that crosses the entire visible world,
   intersecting West Africa as a near-vertical line at ~0°. Fixing the
   antimeridian crossings (decision 2 below) eliminates the West Africa
   artifact as a side effect.

2. **Side panel is messy.** "Looks like an amateur designed it. There's
   a bottom scroll on the panel that shouldn't be there." Concretely, the
   v1.2 `.side-panel` is a vertical flexbox with `gap: 12px`,
   `padding: 16px`, `overflow-y: auto`. Its children stack as:
   `<LanguagePicker>` (~60px), `<LanguageList>` (32px × N rows),
   `<StatsPanel>` (~140px, two stacked cards), `<LevelRangeSlider>`
   (~100px including the rc-slider track + two `<select>` fallbacks).
   At 1024×768 with 8 languages the contents total ≈ 60 + 32·8 + 140 +
   100 + 7·12 (gaps) + 32 (padding) = **672px** of content on a 768px
   viewport — and the rc-slider's `<select>` fallback duplicates levels
   horizontally below the slider, which is what produces the **horizontal**
   scroll bar at 360px panel width. There is no section structure, no
   typography hierarchy, and `LanguageList`'s `<li>` elements wrap when a
   long language name (Scottish Gaelic, Luxembourgish, Kinyarwanda) plus
   "— C1 Remove" exceeds the 360px row.

PRD v1.3 (`docs/PRD.md` §v1.3, AC v1.3-1 through v1.3-9) and the approved
plan (`C:/Users/alexa/.claude/plans/my-language-map-is-polished-hollerith.md`
§v1.3) capture the user-visible scope. This ADR locks the technical
decisions and orders the tasks.

## Decision

1. **Antarctica: drop entirely from `countries.geo.json`.** No language in
   `LANGUAGES` has `"AQ"` in its `regions[]`; no entry in
   `must-include.json` references AQ; no admin-1 polygon depends on AQ.
   Dropping the feature in `scripts/build-geo.ts` (a one-line `if (iso ===
   "AQ") continue;`) costs zero product surface and removes the south-edge
   horizontal band entirely. Re-adding AQ later, if a future cycle wants
   to highlight English/French/Russian research-station coverage, is a
   one-line revert plus an ADR addendum. Re-adding it cleanly would
   require either splitting the polygon at the antimeridian and clipping
   it to a sensible north edge, or switching to a 110m source — both of
   which are out of scope for v1.3.

2. **Antimeridian-crossing polygons: split rings at ±180° in
   `scripts/build-geo.ts`.** Add a hand-written splitter to the build
   script. The splitter walks every ring of every Polygon / MultiPolygon
   feature, detects edges where consecutive points have `Δlon > 180°`
   (i.e. the edge crosses the antimeridian the "wrong way"), and splits
   the ring into two: one ring with all points clamped to ≤ 180° (the
   eastern hemisphere half), one with all points clamped to ≥ -180° (the
   western half). Crossing edges are interpolated to land on ±180° at the
   correct latitude. This converts MultiPolygons like Russia's Chukotka
   ring (which runs from `~178°E` west through `-179°W`) into two
   non-crossing rings — one at 178°…180°, one at -180°…-179° — which
   Leaflet renders correctly as two separate polygons.

   We **do not add a runtime dependency** for this. The splitter is ~40
   lines of TypeScript local to `scripts/build-geo.ts`. Pulling in
   `@turf/turf` (~3MB), `polygon-clipping`, or `geojson-antimeridian-cut`
   for one build-time pass is overkill — the build script already imports
   `topojson-client` for the same reason and the dataset is regenerated
   only when `world-atlas` is updated. The splitter is pure-functional
   and has clear unit-test surface (a small `tests/unit/build-geo.test.ts`
   spec exercises the four edge cases: pure-east ring, pure-west ring,
   single-crossing ring, double-crossing ring). After running, every ring
   in `countries.geo.json` has `lonSpan ≤ 180°`.

3. **West Africa stray lines: no direct fix needed; verified as a
   side-effect of decision 2.** The investigation in §Context confirms no
   West Africa feature contains a sliver ring. The reported lines come
   from Russia's antimeridian artifacts visually projecting through the
   region. v1.3-2's acceptance criterion ("no thin north-south line
   outside legitimate borders") is satisfied transitively by decision 2.
   QA's manual verification step explicitly walks the West Africa region
   at default zoom after the build-geo regeneration, and the e2e test for
   v1.3-1 (no full-width thin path) covers the same artifact class.

4. **Side panel layout: CSS Grid with four named sections, internal
   list-only scroll, ellipsis truncation.**

   - **Internal grid.** `.side-panel` becomes
     `display: grid; grid-template-rows: auto 1fr auto auto;
     grid-template-columns: 1fr; gap: 16px; padding: 16px; height: 100vh;
     min-height: 0; overflow: hidden`. The four rows are, top to bottom:
     **add** (auto), **list** (`1fr` — takes remaining space), **stats**
     (auto), **filter** (auto). The panel itself never scrolls; the list
     row is the only flex-grow / scrollable region. `overflow: hidden` on
     the panel kills both the horizontal scroll bar from the rc-slider
     fallback selects and the vertical scroll bar from content overflow.
     `min-height: 0` on both the panel and the list row is the standard
     CSS-grid escape hatch that lets the inner `<ul>` actually shrink and
     scroll instead of pushing the panel's content past its bounds.

   - **Section markers.** Four `<section>` elements wrap the existing
     four panel components in `App.tsx`, each with a stable `data-testid`
     per PRD v1.3-6: `panel-section-add`, `panel-section-list`,
     `panel-section-stats`, `panel-section-filter`. Each section has an
     `<h2>` title ("Add a language", "Your languages", "Stats", "Filter
     by level") and a thin top divider (`border-top: 1px solid #2a2f37`,
     CSS class `.panel-section`) — except the first section, which omits
     the divider. **No new component file.** A `<PanelSection>` wrapper
     would be 5 lines of JSX with no state, used in exactly one place;
     inlining the four `<section data-testid=…>` blocks in `App.tsx` is
     simpler and consistent with the v1 / v1.1 pattern of plain semantic
     wrappers under `<aside>`.

   - **Typography palette.** Add a small block of CSS variables and
     rules in `src/styles.css`:
     ```
     --panel-title:    600 12px/1.2 system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
     --panel-row:      400 14px/1.4 system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
     --panel-badge:    600 11px/1   system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
     --panel-stat-num: 600 22px/1.1 system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
     --panel-stat-lbl: 400 11px/1.2 system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
     ```
     Section `<h2>` uses `var(--panel-title)` with `text-transform:
     uppercase; letter-spacing: 0.04em; color: var(--muted)`. Language
     row name uses `var(--panel-row)`. Level badge (the `— C1` text in
     v1.2) becomes a styled `<span class="level-badge">` using
     `var(--panel-badge)`, `padding: 2px 6px`, `border-radius: 4px`,
     `background: #1f2937`. Stats numbers use `var(--panel-stat-num)`,
     stat labels use `var(--panel-stat-lbl)`.

   - **Spacing.** `padding: 16px` on the panel, `gap: 16px` on the
     panel grid, `gap: 8px` on the list `<ul>`, `margin-bottom: 8px` on
     each section `<h2>`. No other custom margins.

   - **Stats card layout.** `StatsPanel`'s outer `<div>` becomes a
     `display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap: 12px` so the
     two cards (Communicate / Get by) sit side-by-side instead of
     stacked. Each card is `padding: 10px 12px; border-radius: 6px;
     background: #161b22`. This saves ~80px of vertical space — enough
     by itself to clear the 768px target with 8 languages — and reads
     better at a glance because the user can compare B1+ vs A1+ side
     by side. Card markup unchanged inside (testids preserved); only
     the outer wrapper class changes.

   - **Long-name truncation in `LanguageList`.** Each `<li>` becomes
     `display: flex; align-items: center; gap: 8px; min-width: 0`. The
     name `<span>` becomes `flex: 1; min-width: 0; overflow: hidden;
     text-overflow: ellipsis; white-space: nowrap`. The level badge
     `<span>` and the remove `<button>` stay `flex: 0 0 auto` so they
     never shrink. At 360px panel width minus 32px padding minus 8px
     gap × 2 minus ~36px badge minus ~60px button = ~216px for the
     name, which fits "Scottish Gaelic" (15 chars) comfortably; the
     longest dataset name (currently "Standard Mandarin Chinese" at 25
     chars) gets ellipsis truncation as designed.

## Consequences

- `scripts/build-geo.ts` grows by ~50 lines (the antimeridian splitter
  and the AQ skip) and gains a small unit test in
  `tests/unit/build-geo.test.ts`.
- `src/data/countries.geo.json` is regenerated and shrinks by 109 rings
  (Antarctica) and grows by ~4 rings (Russia/Fiji split halves). Net file
  size change ≈ -8KB.
- `src/App.tsx` grows by ~15 lines (four `<section data-testid=…>`
  wrappers + four `<h2>` titles).
- `src/styles.css` grows by ~80 lines (panel grid, section, typography
  palette, stats grid, list row truncation, level badge style).
- `src/components/LanguageList.tsx` and `src/components/StatsPanel.tsx`
  pick up small markup tweaks (level-badge span, stats outer grid
  class). Public props unchanged.
- No new runtime dependencies. No new dev dependencies (the splitter is
  hand-written; e2e specs use the existing Playwright + Leaflet setup).
- `world-atlas` stays on `countries-50m.json` (no source swap).
- A v1.2 user reloading after deploying v1.3 keeps their saved languages
  intact. Visual changes are limited to the map (Antarctica gone,
  Russia/Fiji properly split) and the side panel (sections, typography,
  side-by-side stats).

## Follow-ups (recorded for the final architectural check)

- **Antarctica re-introduction.** If a future cycle wants to color the
  research-station coverage on AQ, the work is: (a) keep the AQ feature
  in `build-geo.ts`; (b) clip its geometry to a sensible north edge
  (e.g. -60° latitude) so it doesn't render as a horizontal band; (c)
  rerun the antimeridian splitter (which already handles AQ's
  crossings). Recorded for v1.4+ if needed.
- **Splitter scope is hemispheric, not pole-aware.** The splitter splits
  rings at ±180° lon but does not handle "rings that enclose a pole"
  (which the world-atlas dataset doesn't ship — Antarctica is dropped
  here, and no other feature winds around a pole). If a future dataset
  swap introduces a pole-enclosing ring, the splitter will need a
  pole-aware extension; not in scope today.
- **Stats card density vs. populated tooltip text.** The side-by-side
  stats grid leaves less room for the existing "ⓘ" tooltip text. If the
  tooltip overflows the card on long localized strings, the implementer
  may need to switch from the current inline `<span role="tooltip">` to
  a positioned popover. Decision deferred to the implementer pass; if
  the existing inline approach overflows, raise it as a v1.3 follow-up
  rather than blocking the cycle.
- **The dataset still has 139 entries against the 250 target.** Carried
  forward from ADR 0003. Out of scope for v1.3.
- **Section dividers: gap-only, no `border-top`.** §4 of this ADR drafted
  thin `border-top: 1px solid #2a2f37` dividers between panel sections.
  The implementation chose a 16px grid `gap` instead and the architectural
  check accepted it: against the dark `#0e1116` panel the uppercase muted
  section titles plus 16px whitespace already read as four distinct
  sections without adding visual noise from divider lines. Recorded as
  the accepted approach for v1.3+; revisit only if user feedback says the
  sections feel undermarked.
- **Inline typography values, no `--panel-*` CSS variables.** §4 of this
  ADR drafted five `--panel-title` / `--panel-row` / `--panel-badge` /
  `--panel-stat-num` / `--panel-stat-lbl` custom properties. The
  implementation inlined the `font-size` / `font-weight` / `line-height`
  / `color` values directly in `.side-panel section h2`,
  `.language-row-name`, `.level-badge`, `.stats-card-count`, and
  `.stats-card-label`. Each value is used in exactly one place; vars
  would add indirection without reuse. Accepted as the cleaner choice;
  introduce vars later only if a second consumer appears.
